Adversarial Evaluation Framework for Cross-Modal Dependency in Vision-Language Models

(2026) — arXiv:

What this paper claims

  • The LoRA Optimized configuration reduces average accuracy Drop under adversarial text perturbation from 27.5% to 9.8% (64.4% relative improvement, p<0.001) compared to baseline CLIP ViT-B/32 on a geometric shapes dataset.
  • The LoRA Optimized model maintains 97% normal accuracy while substantially improving adversarial robustness.
  • The optimization achieves greater than 60% improvement across all four adversarial strategies, indicating broad reduction in text dependency rather than strategy-specific gains.
  • The combined Drop reduction of 17.7 percentage points exceeds the expected 8-12% sum of individual component contributions, indicating synergistic effects among optimization techniques.
  • Color swap adversarial attacks show the highest improvement rate (68%) while position swap attacks remain the most challenging despite 60% improvement.

LoRA: Low-Rank Adaptation of Large Language Models

Edward J. Hu, Yelong Shen, Phillip Wallis, Zeyuan Allen-Zhu, Yuanzhi Li, Shean Wang, Lu Wang, Weizhu Chen (2022) — arXiv:2106.09685

What this paper claims

  • LoRA matches or exceeds full fine-tuning performance on RoBERTa, DeBERTa, GPT-2, and GPT-3 despite training far fewer parameters.
  • LoRA introduces zero additional inference latency compared to adapter-based methods which increase latency by up to 30%.
  • A very low rank (r=1 or r=2) suffices for LoRA adaptation even when the full weight matrix rank is 12,288.
  • Adapting both Wq and Wv yields better performance than adapting a single weight type with higher rank given the same parameter budget.
  • The adaptation matrix delta-W amplifies task-specific directions not emphasized in the pre-trained weight matrix W rather than repeating its top singular directions.
  • LoRA reduces trainable parameters by 10,000x and GPU memory by 3x compared to full fine-tuning on GPT-3 175B.

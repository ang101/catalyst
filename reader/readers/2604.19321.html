RDP LoRA: Geometry-Driven Identification for Parameter-Efficient Adaptation in Large Language Models

Yusuf Çelebi, Yağız Asker, Özay Ezerceli, Mahmoud ElHussieni, Selva Taş, Reyhan Bayraktar, Fatma Betül Terzioğlu (2026) — arXiv:

What this paper claims

  • Adapting only 13 RDP-selected layers of Qwen3-8B-Base with LoRA achieves 81.67% on MMLU-Math, significantly outperforming full 36-layer LoRA adaptation at 79.32%.
  • Geometry-selected sparse LoRA consistently outperforms random sparse LoRA with the same number of adapted layers, demonstrating that RDP captures a structurally meaningful signal for layer selection.
  • Layer identity matters more than the number of adapted layers or capacity allocation strategy, as uniform capacity on geometrically selected layers outperforms weighted capacity variants.
  • The geometry-based layer selection method generalizes across model scales and architectures, matching or exceeding Full LoRA on Qwen3-14B and DeepSeek-LLM-7B with fewer parameters.

Sentence-BERT: Sentence Embeddings using Siamese BERT-Networks

Nils Reimers, Iryna Gurevych (2019) — arXiv:1908.10084

What this paper claims

  • SBERT sentence embeddings trained on NLI data significantly outperform InferSent and Universal Sentence Encoder on semantic textual similarity tasks
  • Averaging BERT output or using the CLS token produces sentence embeddings worse than average GloVe embeddings for cosine-similarity-based STS tasks
  • SBERT reduces the time to find the most similar sentence pair among 10,000 sentences from approximately 65 hours with BERT to about 5 seconds
  • SBERT achieves state-of-the-art performance on SentEval transfer tasks compared to other sentence embedding methods
  • Replacing BERT with RoBERTa does not yield significant improvement in sentence embedding quality
  • The element-wise difference |u-v| is the most important concatenation component for the classification objective function

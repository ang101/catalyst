Sentence-BERT: Sentence Embeddings using Siamese BERT-Networks

Nils Reimers, Iryna Gurevych (2019) — arXiv:1908.10084

What this paper claims

  • SBERT sentence embeddings trained on NLI data significantly outperform InferSent and Universal Sentence Encoder on unsupervised STS tasks as measured by Spearman correlation with cosine similarity.
  • Directly using BERT output (averaged embeddings or CLS token) as sentence embeddings with cosine similarity yields worse performance than average GloVe embeddings on STS tasks.
  • SBERT fine-tuned on NLI then STSb achieves higher Spearman correlation on STSb than models trained on STSb alone.
  • Replacing BERT with RoBERTa does not yield significant improvement in sentence embedding quality.
  • SBERT with smart batching on GPU is faster than InferSent and Universal Sentence Encoder for computing sentence embeddings.
  • The element-wise difference |u-v| is the most important concatenation component for the classification objective function in SBERT.
  • SBERT sentence embeddings achieve state-of-the-art average performance on SentEval transfer tasks compared to InferSent and Universal Sentence Encoder.
